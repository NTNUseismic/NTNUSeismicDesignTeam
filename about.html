About the Competition

Understanding the EERI Seismic Design Competition

What is the Seismic Design Competition?

The annual Undergraduate Seismic Design Competition (SDC), organized by EERI's Student Leadership Council, challenges students to design and construct a cost-effective and architecturally innovative earthquake-resistant structure.

Competition Overview

Teams from universities worldwide compete to design, analyze, and construct a scaled balsa wood model of a multi-story building. The structure must meet strict requirements while demonstrating innovation in earthquake-resistant design.

Competition Phases

1. Design Development

Teams develop their building design considering:

  • Structural efficiency
  • Architectural aesthetics
  • Seismic performance predictions
  • Construction cost estimates

2. Construction

Teams build their designs using:

  • Lasercut balsa wood members
  • Joints and glued connections
  • Seismic dampeners

3. Testing

Structures undergo:

  • Ground motion simulations
  • Performance measurements
  • Displacement monitoring

Evaluation Criteria

Annual Revenue

A function of the rentable floor area with respect to the structural system. Bonuses are given to the teams with the best proposal, architecture, presentation, poster and dampeners.

Annual Building Cost

A function of the weight of the structure. Penalties are given to models who break the structural requirement rules

Annual Seismic Cost

Scored based on the seismic performance on the shake table and prior computer predictions.

Competition Benefits

  • Hands-on experience in earthquake engineering
  • Exposure to industry professionals
  • Development of teamwork and project management skills
  • Understanding of real-world design constraints
  • Networking with international peers

Quick Facts

  • When: Annual competition
  • Where: Different host cities each year
  • Who: University teams worldwide
  • Duration: Year-long preparation
